off the spectrum lays the entire electromagnetic spectrum, and audible sound alongside it, out on one ruler spanning twenty four orders of magnitude, from the hum of household power to cosmic rays. Human hearing and human sight sit on it at their real width and real position, next to a growing set of animals and machines and exactly what each one can and cannot detect. Drag anywhere in the lower half of the window and pay attention to the big panel, and the bars stacked beneath it.

The big panel

It is black almost everywhere. It only fills with an actual color while you are inside the narrow visible band, the same true color your eyes would see at that wavelength. Inside the audible band it shows a moving wave instead, and if you turn Sound on with the speaker button, it plays that exact real frequency, not a transposed stand in. Every other position on the scale genuinely has no color or sound, so the panel stays black rather than faking one.

The bars underneath

Each row is a different creature or, near the bottom, a machine. Some rows hold more than one colored segment when that creature has more than one sense, your own row shows both hearing and sight side by side. A segment only lights up in full color when the ruler's position falls inside it, and hovering over a row lights its segments up too, so you can see exactly where they sit. Most positions light up nothing at all. Watch how rarely any two rows are lit together, and how the machines near the bottom, an ELF receiver, a radio receiver, a thermal camera, a space telescope, an X-ray telescope, a gamma detector, a cosmic ray observatory, cover far more ground together than any living thing on the list, with no real gap between them.

The ruler

The ruler itself is black except for one bright sliver: the visible band, right where your eyes work. Everything else genuinely has no color, so it stays black rather than faking one. The text labels above and below mark real world landmarks, radio, microwave, ultraviolet, and so on, with their real frequency ranges, click one to jump straight there.

The point

Drag slowly from one end to the other. Notice how much of the trip is spent with the panel black and every bar dim. That stretch is most of the scale. What any of us can actually sense, human or animal, is a handful of small, mostly separate slivers of a much larger whole.
