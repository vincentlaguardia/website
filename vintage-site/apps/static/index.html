Static Coefficient (SC Index)
Static reactivity is modeled as generation × retention. Generation (G) is the blend's normalized displacement from the triboelectric neutral point (~66): charge transfer scales with separation on the series, so fibers at either extreme develop larger contact potentials against an arbitrary counter surface. Retention (R) is the reciprocal of charge dissipation capacity, governed by moisture regain. Adsorbed water raises surface conductivity, lowering the charge decay time constant. R = 1/(1 + β·regain·RH/65). Move the humidity slider to see how ambient RH changes R and the final SC value.

Static Coefficient Calculator
Triboelectric position weighted by moisture-dependent charge retention
This calculator estimates a garment's tendency to build and keep electrostatic charge. The score is the product of a generation term from triboelectric displacement and a retention term from moisture regain under ambient humidity.

Equation

SC = 100 × G × R, where G = |tribo − 66| / 40 and R = 1 / (1 + 0.5 × regain × RH / 65)

Interpretation

The blend triboelectric value is the weighted average of the selected fibers. Materials far from the neutral point generate larger contact potentials, so the model assigns them a higher generation score. Moisture lowers surface resistivity, so higher regain and higher humidity reduce retention and pull the final static coefficient downward.
