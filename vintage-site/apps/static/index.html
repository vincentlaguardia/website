a two factor model for the intrinsic static reactivity of textile fabrics

the static coefficient (sc index)
dr. vincent j. pastrami
institute for applied triboelectrics
abstract. the classical triboelectric series ranks materials only in pairs and therefore cannot, on its own, assign a static score to a single garment. we present a two factor model in which a fabric's intrinsic static reactivity is the product of charge generation, its distance from the triboelectric neutral point, and charge retention, its inability to dissipate accumulated charge, governed by moisture regain. the resulting static coefficient (sc index) reproduces the empirically known behaviour of common fibres, cotton calm, polyester volatile, wool self regulating, from first principles, and extends naturally to blends and to varying ambient humidity.

1. the problem with the series

ask which fabric is "most static" and intuition reaches for the triboelectric series, the ranked list of materials by their tendency to gain or lose electrons on contact. but the series is inherently relational: it predicts the charge developed between two surfaces rubbed together, as a function of their separation on the list. it says nothing directly about a lone garment. a single fabric has no triboelectric partner until it meets one, and in daily life it meets many: skin, upholstery, other garments, itself. a useful single fabric metric must therefore characterise a tendency averaged over the many surfaces a garment actually encounters.

2. two competing processes

observed static nuisance, cling, crackle, the doorknob shock, is not governed by charge generation alone. it is the net result of two opposing processes. charge is generated by contact and separation; charge is simultaneously dissipated by conduction away from the surface. a fabric that generates strongly but dissipates instantly is calm in practice; a fabric that generates modestly but cannot shed what it makes becomes a persistent offender. any faithful model must represent both, and multiply them: a garment is troublesome only when it both acquires charge and holds onto it.

3. generation potential (g)

we proxy a fabric's charging tendency by its distance from the triboelectric neutral point, the region of the series (near cotton and steel) where materials neither strongly donate nor strongly accept electrons. a fabric at an extreme of the series sits far from most surfaces it will meet, and large separations yield large charge transfer; a near neutral fabric sits close to most surfaces and charges weakly against them. generation potential is thus defined as the normalised absolute distance of the fabric's series position from neutral, saturating at unity:

g = min(1, gap / 40), where gap is the distance of position from 66

4. retention factor (r)

the dominant control on charge dissipation in textiles is not the dry polymer, which is an excellent insulator, but the thin film of absorbed atmospheric moisture it carries. water provides the conductive pathway along which accumulated charge bleeds away. the relevant material property is therefore moisture regain: the mass of water a conditioned fibre holds relative to its dry mass. hydrophilic fibres (wool ≈ 16%, cotton ≈ 8.5%) drain charge readily; hydrophobic synthetics (polyester ≈ 0.4%) cannot. we model retention, the failure to dissipate, as a saturating inverse function of absorbed moisture:

r = 1 / (1 + β · regain · rh/65)

where the regain value is scaled by ambient relative humidity against the 65% rh standard at which it is defined, and β tunes the strength of moisture's static quenching effect. as humidity rises, even a modestly hygroscopic fabric acquires enough surface water to shed charge, and r falls.

5. formulation

the static coefficient is the product of the two factors, expressed on a 0 to 100 scale:

sc = 100 · g · r

the intrinsic coefficient reported by the instrument evaluates r at a 40% rh indoor dry reference, chosen because it is the regime in which static actually manifests and therefore best exposes a fabric's latent personality. a low sc denotes a fabric that is calm because it barely charges, barely retains, or both; a high sc denotes one that both charges strongly and cannot let go.

6. humidity dependence

because r is a function of ambient humidity, the same garment does not possess a single fixed static value but a response curve. a polyester fleece remains volatile across almost the entire humidity range; a cotton shirt is calm throughout; wool traverses a wide arc, crackling in a heated, dry room yet placid in damp outdoor air. this humidity sensitivity is a feature of the physics, not a modelling artefact, and it explains why static complaints spike in winter, when indoor heating drives relative humidity, and thus fibre moisture, toward zero.

two results deserve comment. nylon sits at the far positive extreme of the series (the strongest generator among garment fibres) yet is unusually hygroscopic for a synthetic; its moderate regain rescues it into the middle of the scale. wool generates heavily but carries the highest regain of any common fibre, and so self regulates, a physical account of why wool feels lively when dry but rarely clings outdoors.

8. blends

for a multi fibre garment, both governing properties, series position and regain, are taken as the mass weighted averages of the constituent fibres, and the coefficient is computed from the blend values. a blend therefore interpolates between its parents: a 60/40 cotton polyester shirt lands between calm cotton and volatile polyester, weighted toward the larger share. this linear composition is a first order approximation; interfacial effects between dissimilar fibres are not modelled.

9. limitations

the model is deliberately reduced. triboelectric ordering is not strictly one dimensional and shifts with surface finish, contaminants, and prior handling; the neutral point is an empirical anchor, not a fundamental constant. fabric construction, knit versus weave, nap, pile, and total contact area, measurably affects real charging but is outside the present scope. the generation from neutral distance step is a defensible modelling choice rather than a directly measured quantity, though every fibre property feeding it is drawn from published data. several minor fibres are positioned by analogy to a listed relative and are flagged accordingly in the instrument.
