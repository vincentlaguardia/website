Techno Mode: A Structured Model of Controlled Visual Conflict in Generative Color Systems
Abstract
Techno Mode is a parametrized color generation regime defined over a fixed 7×7 compositional grid. It is characterized by (1) a restricted subset of admissible palette philosophies, (2) a bimodal saturation and lightness state system (pastel or neon), (3) an expanded hue drift range governed by a normalized "strange" parameter, and (4) a deterministic diagonal band assignment that preserves spatial organization independent of color randomization. This paper presents the formal structure of Techno Mode, derives its governing equations, and argues that the system produces controlled instability: perceptual conflict that is statistically bounded and spatially organized rather than stochastic noise. We formalize the model as controlled instability = structure + deviation, quantify each deviation term, and describe the deterministic diagonal banding rule that underlies the entire system.
1. Introduction
Generative color systems typically face a tradeoff between visual coherence and expressive range. Fully constrained systems (e.g., strict complementary or triadic harmony) produce legible, resolved compositions but limited variation. Fully unconstrained systems (uniform random hue, saturation, lightness) produce high variation but incoherent output resembling noise.
Techno Mode occupies a middle position. It fixes the spatial and structural scaffold of a composition (grid geometry, band assignment, and underlying palette logic) while deliberately increasing the behavioral range of color within that scaffold: which harmony systems are permitted, how far hue may drift, and how sharply saturation and lightness bifurcate into two visual states. The result is termed controlled visual conflict: instability that is real and measurable, but never structurally unmoored.
2. Grid Structure
7 × 7 grid, 49 total cells. Target circle cells: 9 (18.4%). Background or support cells: 40 (81.6%). Because background or support cells dominate, the perceptual field is driven mainly by background color relationships rather than the target circles alone.
3. Palette Philosophy Restriction
Full system: 6 philosophies (monochromatic, analogous, complementary, split complementary, triadic, tetradic). Techno Mode allows 4 (monochromatic, analogous, split complementary, tetradic) and excludes 2 (complementary, triadic).
allowed = 4/6 = 66.7%, excluded = 2/6 = 33.3%
Complementary and triadic are the two most resolved harmony systems: complementary is strictly binary, and triadic is evenly tripartite at 120° intervals. Removing them eliminates the system's most inherently balanced configurations, biasing the remaining palette space toward asymmetric harmonies that tolerate drift without collapsing.
4. Binary Zone Behavior
Pastel: saturation 50 to 80 (mean ≈65), lightness 72 to 88 (mean ≈80). Neon: saturation 92 to 100 (mean ≈96), lightness 47 to 67 (mean ≈57).
The difference between neon and pastel mean saturation is 31 points (96 minus 65). The difference between neon and pastel mean lightness is 23 points (80 minus 57).
These states are not overlapping in saturation and only marginally adjacent in lightness, representing a genuine two population split rather than a continuous gradient.
5. Hue Drift
Minimum spread: 80° (22.2% of the 360° wheel). Maximum spread: 300° (83.3% of the wheel). At the upper bound, a cell's color can migrate far enough to approximate an entirely different palette philosophy in practice, even though its formal harmony assignment doesn't change.
6. The Strange Slider
w equals strange divided by 100, so w falls between 0 and 1.
hue spread as a function of w equals 80° plus 220° times w.
At w = 0: 80°. At w = 1: 300°. Strange is the single control governing hue instability magnitude.
7. Pastel Probability
The pastel condition holds when jitterD is greater than 0.38 plus jitterE times 0.24 times w.
Low strange (w≈0): threshold ≈0.38, which yields pastel ≈62%, neon ≈38%. Max strange (w=1): threshold avg ≈0.50, which yields pastel ≈50%, neon ≈50%.
As strange rises, the split moves from a pastel weighted distribution toward maximum entropy (even 50/50).
8. Spatial Organization: Diagonal Band Assignment
Using zero indexed row r and column c, band membership is defined as: band equals (r plus c) mod N, where N = 4 in the reference configuration.
Cells sharing the same value of r plus c lie on the same diagonal; the modulo operation folds diagonals N apart into shared bands, turning 13 raw diagonals (on a 7×7 grid) into a repeating 4 band cycle. Reading across the grid row by row, row A resolves to bands 1, 2, 3, 4, 1, 2, 3; row B to 2, 3, 4, 1, 2, 3, 4; row C to 3, 4, 1, 2, 3, 4, 1; row D to 4, 1, 2, 3, 4, 1, 2; row E repeats row A's pattern; row F repeats row B's pattern; and row G repeats row C's pattern.
Diagonal (not row or column) banding is used because rows and columns read as mechanically gridded, while diagonals produce a woven, drifting quality while staying fully deterministic.
Two stages, strictly separated: stage one (structure) consists of band assignment with no randomness involved. Stage two (deviation) allows each cell's hue, saturation, and lightness to vary, within its band's permitted range, according to the rules in Sections 3 through 7; randomness never feeds back into stage one. This is why conflict shows up at three levels (cell, neighbor, and grid) without ever destabilizing the underlying grid.
9. Overall Interpretation
controlled instability = structure + deviation
Structure: 7×7 grid, deterministic band rule based on (r plus c) mod N, retained (narrowed) palette logic. Deviation: 33.3% of philosophies excluded, 2 discrete color states separated by about 31 saturation points and 23 lightness points, hue drift spanning 22.2% to 83.3% of the wheel, pastel probability shifting from about 62% toward about 50%.
10. Conclusion
Techno Mode is a structured conflict system, not a stochastic effect. The grid, band assignment, and narrowed palette logic stay fixed on every generation; only how color behaves inside that scaffold varies. Instability is measurable, bounded, and reproducible: a designed tension between a deterministic spatial substrate and a probabilistically expanded space of color behavior.
